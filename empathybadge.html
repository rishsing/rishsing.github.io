Empathy Badge

Generating empathy for blue collar wokers


User Research & User Experience Design, Jan 2016
(4 weeks)

The Challenge

In this project, me and my team members were tasked with designing a wearable that addresses the themes of identity, beliefs, tolerance, and inclusivity. The design prompt was very open. This essentially meant that we, as a team, had the freedom to explore the space and find the problems we wanted to address through our design.

User Research

How we did it ?

Considering how uniform is an integral part of the wearer's life, we decided to focus on uniforms and how they affect an invdividual's identity and his/her interaction with other people. Therefore, for our research:

  • We interviewed a variety of people who wear uniforms on a daily basis: fast food employees, bus drivers & police officers. Based on these interviews we realised that different uniforms evoke different emotions. People form associations based on the nature of work someone's doing. Blue collar workers consequently don't get the respect they deserve.

  • Using our interviews as a jumping off point, we decided to conduct a fly-on-the-wall observation in a food court. Therefore, we sat on a table near the cash counters and food stalls and observed interactions between blue collar workers and the customers they were serving. During this time, we also conducted photo ethnography.



Insights

What we found ?


1. Workers try to personalize their uniforms as much as possible within the constraints given to express their personalities.


2. Uniforms make employees easily identifiable but also take away their individuality.


3. Interactions between customers and employees tend to be fairly limited.





People are indifferent towards blue collar workers and form biases based on the uniform they are wearing and/or the nature of the job they are doing. They see blue collar workers as a part of the system/organization and often forget that they are humans too who, just like them, are working a day job to pay their bills.

So for our solution, we focused on:

Generating empathy by helping customers identify with the employee serving them.

Our Concept



Our concept seeks to generate empathy between employees and customers to create more pleasant interactions. A simple circular name tag tells customers a little bit about the worker's life and facilitates interactions.



What I learned ?

  1. Embracing Chaos: Because the prompt was so open ended, we didn't know where to start. Therefore, using affinity diagramming, we decided to systematically note down pre-dispositions each team member had regarding the given topic. This helped us orient ourselves and gave us a direction of sorts. This was followed by brainstorming sessions and some exploratory research which guided us to some of the potential problems we could address. In the end, we picked one depending on how accessible and easy it would be to do user research in that problem space.

Tools used:      

Firefighter tool design

Asking good questions !

  • What information do you get from dispatch and in what form ?
  • What do you do after you get a call from dispatch ? Why do you follow that process ?
  • What information do you exactly need regarding, say a building fire and how does that help you handle the emergency?
  • How comfortable are you with technology ? What role does technology play in day to day operations ? Do you view technology as a source of information only or can it contribute more to your day to day information ?
  • Do you, as a firefighter, want information specific to your role ?
  • What is the most basic information that you need that you absolutely cannot work without ?
  • What does a typical dispatch message look like ?
