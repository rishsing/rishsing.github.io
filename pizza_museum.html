The Pizza Games

A pizza museum exhibhit that promotes co-experience
among family members & friends !


User Experience Design, March 2016
(6 weeks)

The Challenge

In this project, me and my team members were tasked with designing a museum exhibit that serves the need of all stakeholders and which will be viewed as innovative. We learned some theoretical frameworks in the first half of the semester such as John Dewey's definition of 'an experience' and McCarthy and Wright's 'Threads of an Experience'. We were expected to use these to design the desired experience.

User Research

How we did it ?

When the project began, we wanted to understand how the museum exhibitions of today reel the visitors in and keep them engaged. Therefore, for the purposes of our research, I visited the Field Museum of Natural History and the Art Intstitute of Chicago. As an when I walked around both the museums, I not only noted how interactive the exhibit is and what technologies are being used to make it interactive, but also how people were engaging with the attractions in the exhibit.



Insights

What we found ?


1. Touch screens are being used increasingly to make exhibits interactive.


2. Visitors play a lot with the mechanical buttons and levers in the museum than they do with touchscreens.


3. The museum used the sense of smell to reel visitors in, in one of its exhibitions.



Process



We were given three different assignments throughout the project to disrupt our process and avoid design fixation. The idea was to get to the right design rather than getting the design right. These assigments helped us generate alternatives and allowed us to thoughtfully explore the space.

The topic of our museum was pizza. Pizza is something that people enjoy together with friends and family. With our museum we wanted to enhance this co-experience while at the same time making it fun and informative.

Assignment No.1

EXPERIENCE PROTOTYPING


What it is?
"An experience prototype is any kind of representation, in any medium, that designed to understand, explore or communicate what it might be like to engage with the product, space or system we are designing."
Buchenau, M., & Suri, J. F. (2000, August). Experience prototyping. In Proceedings of the 3rd conference on Designing interactive systems: processes, practices, methods, and techniques (pp. 424-433). ACM.


Our Concept
For experience prototyping, we wanted to test one of our promising concepts. We wanted to use fun and play to enhance the (co) experience of visiting a pizza museum. Hence, for our concept, we decided to have the visitors make their own pizza in a series of steps. The visitor will make use of an interactive display installed in the floor of the museum to do that.



The idea here is to the visitor’s senses and emotions to keep him/her engaged. Almost everyone likes pizza. Therefore, our concept uses the visitor’s curiosity (about pizza from different parts of the world) to reel him/her in and then keep him/her engaged by providing an experience that leverages McCarthy and Wright’s four threads of experience.



What we learned

  • The goal of our exhibit is to have fun and encourage play among both children and adults. This prototype confirmed that this exhibit is fun because it was both fun for us to create and fun to interact with.

  • Originally we had this idea of play within our exhibit and through seeing our prototype in action, we realized that we’re gamifying pizza making. In each step the user has some activity to complete in order to make her pizza.

  • The prototype helped us narrow down the point/core of our exhibit which was to strengthen bonds with families & friends through food & play.


Assignment No.2

Pastiche Scenario


What is it?
Pastiche scenarios draw on fiction as a resource to explore the interior ‘felt-life’ aspects of user experience and the complex social and cultural issues raised by technological innovations.

For the purposes of this assignment I wrote a pastiche scenario on a characted called Joey Tibbiani from Friends. The scenario explores how Joey would interact with our concept. You can read the entire scenario here.

What we learned ?
Writing and then reading our pastiche scenarios we realised that, although the core of our exhibit was to enhance co-experience, our concept in its present form didn't do that at all. The concept was designed in a way that only allowed one person to interact with it at a time. This was a big revelation and we set out to correct our course.

Assignment No.3

Critical Design


What is it?
Critical Design uses speculative design proposals to challenge narrow assumptions, preconceptions and givens about the role products play in everyday life.

What we did?
The first thing we did to help us explore the critical design space was analyze our pastiche scenarios and develop a list of key takeaways from each of them. The biggest takeaway we found was that the goal of our exhibit was to get visitors to interact and play with one another, yet we built an exhibit in which each individual interacted alone, independent of their other group members.

As such, we wanted our critical design to look at interactions between families. We asked ourselves how and why does pizza bring people together? One topic that we all agreed was interesting and worth examining was families who don’t eat together but instead do so in front of the TV or are busy on their phones. Firefighter tool design

Asking good questions !

  • What information do you get from dispatch and in what form ?
  • What do you do after you get a call from dispatch ? Why do you follow that process ?
  • What information do you exactly need regarding, say a building fire and how does that help you handle the emergency?
  • How comfortable are you with technology ? What role does technology play in day to day operations ? Do you view technology as a source of information only or can it contribute more to your day to day information ?
  • Do you, as a firefighter, want information specific to your role ?
  • What is the most basic information that you need that you absolutely cannot work without ?
  • What does a typical dispatch message look like ?
