Design Process

Question. Defamiliarize. Design


Overview


Phase 1: User Research

Whether you are designing something totally new or re-designing an existing product/service, every design is judged on user’s ability to use the product/service in question, as desired. Therefore, to start things off, I begin by understanding what the problem currently is and why does it exist. In this phase, I narrow down the problem space by identifying the root cause of the problem. I do this by:

  • Asking a lot of good questions to the user to understand the ‘what’ (what do they do) and then the ‘why’ (why do they do it).

  • Trying and understanding the user’s workflow and the context they are operating in. This helps in identifying their pain points.

  • Questioning why is there a flawed approach/process in place. There must be a reason.

  • Identifying the way we are going to solve that problem. This gives us (the team) the core of our design.

Phase 2: Ideate, Design, Iterate

In this phase, I try and brainstorm concepts and then weigh it against our core. I pick the concept thats best inline with our core and then go about refining it. I follow a contraction and expansion technique where in I ask a series of question as to how we can provide what the user needs or is asking for. I do all this by keeping the technical and business constraints in mind.

After arriving at a preliminary prototype, I test it with users to see how usable it is. After studying the results of this usability test, I iterate on the design.

This phase requires good team work. Every team member bring something new to the table. Individual viewpoint comes in handy in addressing not the only the problem we are trying to solve through our design but also any side effects that migh result from our proposed solution.


Phase 3: Reflection



After the project is done and dusted, I look back at the entire process and experience to introspect. With the help of my team, I try to understand what I did right and what I could have done better. I also analyse the nature of the problem and compare it with the process we followed to solve it. This helps me to see if there was something that warranted a different approach. In the end, I write about my experience of working in the project in a personal blog. Doing this permamnently etches the lessons learned in the previous project. Reflecting allows me to learn from my experience and grow and makes me better prepared for the next challenge.
